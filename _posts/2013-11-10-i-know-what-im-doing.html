Most of the time you can call me an early adopter. I bought an iPod the first day they were available back in 2001. I'm not gun-shy of upgrading OS X on release days (heck, I *paid* to get my hands on 10.0 beta). So when Mavericks dropped a week or two ago I fired up the App store and dove right in. A lot of people I know wait until at least the first patch, but I <knocking on keyboard> have never had any problems before. The install went just fine, I was a little annoyed at the 'less than a minute', then restart and more waiting, but aside from that I was playing around with 10.9 within an hour. Awesome.

The only problem I have seem to have encountered popped up yesterday while I was at SCNA 2013. I wrote a simple 1 page script that pulls Instagram photos based on a provided tag, and overlays them on a Mapbox map of where they were taken. I wanted to see who was taking photos and using the #scna hashtag so I opened chrome and went to localhost/instagram (not a very clever name I admit). Not found. Hmm. Apache running? Check. Hmm. I suddenly had flashbacks to years ago when I would spend hours chasing my tail trying to get virtual hosts configured properly. My palms started to sweat.

Then I remembered my DocumentRoot setting in /etc/apache2/httpd.conf file by default is not the old Sites directory. Fire up vim, and aha! OS X had defaulted it back to /Library/WebServer/Documents. I commented out that line to remember what the 'default' now is, and simply added DocumentRoot "/Users/cpeak/Sites". Now any static HTML projects in my site directory get served up using localhost/... in the browser.
