Learn with me

This week I learned about cyclist Gino Bartali, who was a renowned Italian cyclist during the second world war, where he used his fame to support the Italian resistance, hiding a Jewish family in his cellar and smuggling documents, saving countless lives. What jumped out most for me was he never boasted about it, long afterwards saying:

The good is done, but it is not said. And certain medals hang on the soul, not on the jacket

I love that quote so much.
